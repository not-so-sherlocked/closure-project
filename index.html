Dear Jani,

There's no clue who is writing this, and I truly wish for this to stay anonymous. I'm just… a nobody. A silent person who saw you once and never forgot.
I first saw you when we were in 8th grade. Back then, my world was quiet — almost like I lived in a space only I knew. And then, you entered that world, unknowingly breaking the silence I had wrapped myself in.
I was never the talkative type. I barely spoke to anyone — just watched people from afar, too shy to ever step closer. I've always been an introvert, someone who finds it hard to talk, even harder to express.
And one day, a long time later, you smiled at me for no reason — while I was walking with my friend. I took it as a casual smile. But he didn't. He sparked a thought of you in my mind that never really faded.
For the past six years, I've quietly carried that flicker. Somewhere in between, I got a beautiful opportunity to connect with you — a rare, unexpected moment, like a thread the universe dropped between us. But as always, I stayed silent. Not because I didn't want to talk, but because I didn't know how to. And I guess, neither did you.
You probably don't remember me. You probably never even noticed me. That's okay.
Still, I know a lot about you — not in a creepy way, but out of curiosity, admiration. I followed your journey from a distance. I watched your stories. I saw your smile again.
And though I found ways to know more about you, I had chances to say hi. I didn't. Not because I feared rejection. But because I feared breaking whatever fragile, imagined version of you I carried in my mind. And maybe a little because of my ego. Or fear of being just another forgettable face.
I know how this sounds — strange, maybe even unsettling. But I promise, this isn't some twisted love letter. It's more like… closure. For me.
Because after six years, I want to stop thinking about what could've been — and start making peace with what wasn't.
That's why I'm writing this. To stop. To move on.
But before that, I needed to tell you this — not because I expect a reply, not because I want you to feel something, but because this is the only way I can free myself.
And maybe, just maybe — if you ever read this, and if any word even slightly touches you, drop a reply — even just a "👍" — just to let me feel like I made a tiny ripple in your world.
All I ever wanted was to send this thought out into the universe. I honestly don't know where I found the courage to send this. Maybe it's madness. Maybe it's healing. Or maybe it's just time.
This isn't a confession. This isn't a proposal. This is just a quiet goodbye to a story that never began — but lived in someone's heart for far too long.
Wishing you peace, freedom, and everything beautiful you deserve. And thank you — for unknowingly being part of someone's universe. Someone you may never remember, but who'll always remember you.
A stranger with a quiet heart
